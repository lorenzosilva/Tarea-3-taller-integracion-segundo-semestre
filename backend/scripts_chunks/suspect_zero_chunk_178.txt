Ted Bundy. He might be driving his car, or having a beer or brushing his teeth... or cutting someone's eyes out. That was said flatly, matter-of-factly. It's chilling. O'RYAN (CONT'D) If you did it right, you got all of it: The way it sounded. The way it smelled... Those people were looking up at you, begging you for mercy. It was like being God. (a beat) ...except you're not. Because you can't do a thing for them. Can't make it stop. You're just watching, helpless. He pauses. O'RYAN (CONT'D) They wired us into this current, the five of us: darkness, the pit itself. We were plugged right into it. But nobody taught us how to shut it off... (thinking back...) We were just men. And we saw things men shouldn't see. Agony, torture, evil - and it never shut off. Even now, it's still there.
